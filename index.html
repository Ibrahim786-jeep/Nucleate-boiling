Introduction

  • Nucleate boiling is an efficient mode of heat transfer where vapor bubbles form and detach from a heated surface. Its performance depends heavily on surface characteristics such as roughness, wettability, and geometry.
  • Inspired by natural surfaces (lotus leaf, shark skin, leaf veins), this study examines how bio-inspired micro-patterns enhance boiling heat transfer, focusing on bubble dynamics, heat transfer coefficient (HTC), and critical heat flux (CHF).

Key Insights:

  • The integration of machine learning/data-driven models with classical boiling physics is an emerging trend.
  • Thin surface geometries, wettability, roughness, and microstructure all interplay to influence bubble dynamics and heat transfer.
  • Existing correlations often lack generality; there is a need for models that link geometry parameters directly to bubble behavior.

Research Gaps & Problem Statement

  • Gap 1: No systematic link between Bio-inspired pattern geometry and boiling parameters such as bubble frequency, HTC, CHF, bubble dynamics, Surface temperature (thermocouples), Visualization of nucleation site density.
  • Gap 2: Lack of research on nucleate boiling behavior over bio-inspired microstructured surfaces, leaving their potential heat transfer enhancement mechanisms unexplored.
  • Gap 3: Benchmark the performance of the bio-inspired surface against existing coated surfaces to evaluate the enhancement in nucleate boiling characteristics.
  • Gap 4: Limited understanding of how bio-inspired microstructured surfaces perform in real-life applications.
  • Problem Statement: Conventional surfaces limit bubble departure and rewetting, causing premature transition to film boiling.

Research Objectives

  1. Design and fabricate bio-inspired micro-patterns (lotus, shark, leaf geometries).
  2. Study boiling heat transfer characteristics on each surface.
  3. Measure bubble site density, departure diameter, HTC, Bubble dynamics (high-speed camera), Surface temperature (thermocouples).
  4. Develop correlations linking pattern geometry to performance.
  5. Benchmark against smooth and coated surfaces.

Methodology

  • Fabrication: Micro-machining of bio-inspired geometries.
  • Characterization: Contact angle, roughness measurement.
  • Pool Boiling Test: Controlled heat flux (50–200 kW/m²), distilled water.
  • Data: Surface temperature, heat flux, bubble dynamics (high-speed camera).
  • Analysis: Derive HTC, CHF, bubble frequency; compare with other coated models (surfaces).

Significance

Significance: Bio-inspired surface micro-patterns enhance boiling by increasing nucleation site density, improving liquid rewetting, and reducing surface superheat. This leads to higher Heat Transfer Coefficient (HTC) and Critical Heat Flux (CHF), improving energy efficiency and system safety.

Area of Application:

  • Electronics cooling and data centers for efficient heat removal.
  • Power generation and nuclear reactors for high heat dissipation.
  • Heat exchangers, HVAC, and desalination systems for optimized thermal management.
  • Aerospace and automotive systems requiring compact, high-performance cooling.

Need of Research: Conventional smooth or coated surfaces have limited nucleation sites and poor rewetting, leading to early vapor film formation. Bio-inspired patterned surfaces mimic natural geometries that enhance capillary action, liquid supply, and vapor removal. There is a strong need to quantify how pattern geometry affects bubble dynamics, HTC, and CHF, to develop predictive models for next-generation high-efficiency boiling surfaces.
